Congratulations to Iniakpokeikiye Thompson on Passing His PhD Viva!

8 April 2026

Congratulations to Iniakpokeikiye (Peter) Thompson, who passed his PhD viva with minor corrections! Peter's thesis focuses on developing an AI app to encourage safer driving in Nigeria — building on similar work previously done in the UK, and exploring the unique challenges and opportunities that arise in a very different cultural and road safety context.

Peter evaluated his app with real drivers and observed a 20% decrease in unsafe driving incidents — a very exciting result with huge real-world potential. Traffic accidents claim significantly more lives in Nigeria than in the UK, making this work particularly impactful. He has now secured post-PhD funding to continue enhancing real-world deployment, and has begun collaborating with interested parties in the neighbouring country of Cameroon.

Our sincere thanks to the examiners, Wei Zhao and Prof. Rita Orji, and to co-supervisor Prof. Ehud Reiter for their support throughout Peter's PhD journey.

WINS-AID Project Awarded UK–India Industry-Academia TNE Funding

18 October 2025

We are delighted to share that WINS-AID (Women IN STEM – AI and Digital Skills) has been awarded funding through the UK–India Industry-Academia TNE Grants 2025–26 by the British Council.

The project is led by Dr. S. M. Riazul Islam (University of Aberdeen, UK), in collaboration with Prof. Ramalingam Murugan (Vellore Institute of Technology, India) and Devendra Gour (Technogaze Solutions Pvt. Ltd., Bhopal). WINS-AID aims to empower women in STEM through targeted training in AI and digital skills, mentorship, and industry-led practical experiences — fostering innovation and building a sustainable pipeline of skilled women professionals for the digital economy.

We look forward to seeing the meaningful impact this initiative will have.
